# Literature Review

Financial markets have evolved significantly, transitioning toward fully data-driven investment strategies. While IPOs consistently attract broad investor participation due to high perceived returns, predicting exact launch performance demands the synthesis of extensive variables across both mathematical fundamentals and institutional sentiment. 

## 1. Existing Tools and Generative AI Gaps
Legacy analysis approaches generally rely on statically computed averages or single-factor assessments (such as viewing only P/E ratios in a vacuum). Recently, retail analysts have attempted to use Large Language Models (LLMs) to forecast listings. However, generative tools heavily rely on predictive phrasing and repeatedly fail to capture actual nonlinear regression curves, leading to confidently inaccurate mathematical outputs. 

## 2. Machine Learning Ensembles in Financial Prediction
Modern supervised learning indicates that ensemble methods outperform traditional linear approximations by synthesizing variant logic trees. Rather than utilizing deep neural networks—which often act as "black box" generators prone to overfitting sparse financial market data—Tree-based ensemble models like Random Forest and Gradient Boosting map out extreme value constraints efficiently. When integrated alongside standard Linear Regression thresholds, these combinations secure the prediction against severe single-model outliers, optimizing listing forecasting without sacrificing interpretability.

## 3. RAG and Financial Document Processing
A major blocker in IPO analysis is the inaccessibility of the 'Red Herring Prospectus' (RHP)—often hundreds of pages long. Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques historically struggle to parse complex multi-column PDFs inherently. However, integrating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) directly into localized PDF mapping parsers allows conversational frameworks to search locally indexed vectors (such as 'Risk Factors', 'Financial Status', 'Business Overviews'). This localized querying severely limits text-based AI from inventing unverified background contexts regarding a business.

## 4. Influential Listing Gain Determinants 
Academic literature validates that early oversubscription velocities inherently signal broader market sentiment. Breaking demand into Institutional (QIB), Non-Institutional (NII), and Retail tiers offers strong predictors of launch momentum natively. Combining these demand curves with historical valuation limits constructs a mathematically robust indicator of day-one percentage fluctuations. 

By analyzing these fields, StockTraQ bridges the verified success of Random Forest numeric regressions with structural RAG ingestion tools, delivering an interface devoid of traditional analytical pitfalls.
